Design notes & open questions
What I'd want to validate next.
Notes
Decisions
  • Section letters are capital, colored, reusable across the form (A appears 3× in ABAB A).
  • Each unique letter has a consistent color app-wide (A=coral, B=teal…).
  • Tempo inheritance: override shown bold + dark, inherited shown faint.
  • Time signature rendered vertically like sheet music (4 over 4).
  • Stanza editing is always inline — no modals or sheets.
  • RUN button persistent at bottom; triggers DAW + click.
  • Song switcher lives in the hamburger drawer; song-form switcher = tabs.
Open questions
  • Where does "section repeat ×N" get edited — on the section card, or as a modifier in the form string?
  • Do stanzas also need repeat counts, or only sections?
  • Should changing Section B's stanzas in one occurrence update ALL B occurrences in the form? (I've assumed yes — one definition, many placements.)
  • What's the keyboard like for the form-string editor in WF2 — a custom letter pad, or regular keyboard?
  • Do we need a "section library" view for sections not currently in the form?
Recommendation
My pick

WF1 (vertical stack) as the base, borrowing the typeable-string header from WF2.

WF1 gives the clearest overview of stanzas within sections — critical since stanzas are where time-sig complexity lives.

But your "edit the pattern by typing" idea is too good to lose, so put it as an editable mini-strip at the top (like WF2's hero).

WF3's proportional blocks are beautiful but too cramped on a phone for dense songs. Could become a "zoom-out" mode.

WF4 is nice for deep per-section editing but loses the song-level picture.
